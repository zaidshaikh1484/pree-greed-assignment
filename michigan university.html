MICHIGAN UNIVERSITY

M UNIVERSITY OF MICHIGAN

  • The University of Michigan (U-M) is a large public research university with campuses in Ann Arbor, Dearborn, and Flint
    known for strong academics, vibrant student life with numerous clubs, and successful athletics, offering diverse programs from
    Engineering to the Arts, and boasts a large student body with high retention, making it a significant institution in Michigan and nationally.

Ann Arbor Campus (Fall 2024 Data)

  • Enrollment: Largest campus, with over 34,000 undergraduates and 18,000 graduate students.

  • Demographics: Diverse student body with significant representation of White, Asian, Hispanic,
    and Black students, plus a large international population.

Dearborn & Flint Campuses (Fall 2024 Data)

  • Dearborn: Around 6,000 undergraduates and 1,900 graduate students.

  • Flint: About 5,000 undergraduates and 1,500 graduate students, with a focus on regional needs.

Michigan State University (For comparison, often confused with U-M)

  • Founded: 1855 as the first land-grant college in the U.S., focusing on agricultural education.

  • In essence, U-M is a major educational powerhouse with a rich history, comprehensive offerings, and a large,
    engaged student body across its Michigan locations, particularly the flagship Ann Arbor campus.
